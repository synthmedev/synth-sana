Synocorp is dead, Long live Me!

Code released into Public Domain.

Synth Me may have gone down with the crash, but it lives on with you. The biometric libraries for the application have been made freely available, along with the code to help developers like you work with the assets. All of it can be downloaded and used by anyone, for any purpose. (But please: Use it for good.)

On behalf of Sana Antariksa, the app’s founder and head developer, the Synth Me team has relinquished its ownership of copyright over these numerous assets in the hopes that they help others in their endeavours to gain ownership of their own body data, away from the corporate eye. Build on our legacy by creating wonderful things of your own agency.

About Synth Me


Synth Me was an application born to be a fun and creative innovation to let any persons to enhance their daily experiences through an exploration of their own body, identity and selfhood. By personal consent, the app was theirs to use. Aspects of our lives through the device- patterns, pictures, videos, all forms of data- gathered to form another version of yourself. The community was diverse and passionate, creating social scenarios beyond what social media could have in this age.

Unfortunately, the synth crash made the application less commercially viable. But all good things come to an end. We hope that by releasing its technology to the public domain, we can see Synth Me live on.

About the team

The Synth Me team was a little rebellious group of developers who passed ethical bylaws by letting themselves be part of a larger cause. As an experimental yet successful collective, they have gone their separate ways, but continue to contribute to a growing Create Me hub.

Create Me is where all your ideas can come together. It has been used by a large number of influencers, tech wizards and You, in making the world a less morbidly surveilled place.

The Goods

The files in this section are packaged as development assets for software developers. You must be running Adobe Flash CS5 or later to open and use them.

More Information...

What is in the packages?

  • The SWF zip files contain compiled versions of everything in the FLA zip files.
  • The GitHub repositories contain everything in the FLA and SWF zip files. They also contain various bits of art and animation that didn't fit neatly into one of the zipped-up categories.

If you are not a developer with access to Adobe CS, don't worry! Get help from your developer friends! Synth Me assets should be handled together.
